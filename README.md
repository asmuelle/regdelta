## Concept

A living, citation-pinned obligation map for SME compliance teams: an agent watches regulators in your jurisdictions daily and maintains an always-current, audit-ready wiki of what changed, who it affects, and what you must do by when — at 1/30th of enterprise RCM pricing.

## Target User & Payer

Compliance officers and ops leads running 1-3 person compliance functions at SME fintechs, healthtech companies, lenders, crypto custodians, and accounting firms covering multiple states. The clearest underserved budget-holder found: a ~$1.1B RCM market growing 8.7-15.8% CAGR where 44% still track manually, and a missed effective date is a personal, career-level liability.

## Auto-Research Mechanic (the living document + delta engine)

Onboarding compiles a company profile (products x jurisdictions x license types) into a monitored primary-source set: Federal Register, agency dockets and guidance (CFPB, FinCEN, OCC, SEC, HHS/OCR, FDA), state regulator bulletins, legislature trackers, EUR-Lex/FCA for cross-border, enforcement-action feeds. Daily content-hash diffing of structured, crawl-friendly government sources; cheap-model triage classifies applicability against the company profile; frontier pass updates topic-scoped living articles ('BNPL lending — California'), each obligation node carrying pinned source text, effective date, comment deadline, and full version history. Deltas ship as change cards with a redline against prior rule text, affected products, required action, and deadline; an entailment check runs before any edit publishes. High-materiality items alert immediately; the rest lands in a weekly digest. Full versioned change history exports as an examiner-facing audit log.

## Product Surface

Web SaaS as system of record (obligation map, redline diffs, immutable change log, PDF/CSV audit-trail export — the artifact compliance buyers show examiners) with Slack and email delta alerts.

## Why Now (2026 timing)

14,000+ regulatory updates/year with accelerating state-level fragmentation (privacy, AI rules, money-transmitter licensing) make a 1-person manual function impossible. Incumbent RCM is quote-only with zero self-serve tier and zero cheap challengers found. Government primary sources are openly crawlable — immune to the publisher-blocking wall (Cloudflare pay-per-crawl, RSL, 79% of top news sites blocking bots) strangling general-web agents.

## Proposed Monetization

$199/mo Core (one regulatory domain, 3 jurisdictions), $499/mo Multi-state (15 jurisdictions, Slack alerts, audit export), $999/mo Firm (fractional-compliance consultancies and accounting firms, multi-client, white-label digests). ~$5-15/customer/mo COGS on free government feeds = 90%+ gross margin, squarely inside the validated $100-500/mo SME compliance budget.

## Competition & Gap

Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence, Compliance.ai, Ascent, LexisNexis State Net — all enterprise quote-only, sold to banks. Below them: generic law-firm client alerts and DIY Google Alerts. ChatGPT/Perplexity disqualified by one-shot statelessness, no effective-date tracking, and citation error rates fatal in compliance. Nothing exists in the $100-500/mo slot.

## Claimed Moat

(1) Vertical trust as product: a compliance officer cannot cite ChatGPT to an examiner; pinned primary-source provenance plus versioned redlines ARE the deliverable, and a regenerating chatbot can never serve as an audit trail. (2) The accumulated 'we detected rule X on date Y and acted' record is the switching cost. (3) Jurisdiction adapters (50 states x multiple agencies, each with its own formats) are grinding work no horizontal will do for a niche. (4) The applicability classifier mapping rules to company profiles improves with every customer in a vertical — proprietary data accumulation.

# Evaluation (multi-agent adversarial review)

## Monetization Analysis — score 7/10

RegDelta scores a 7: a real, verified ~$1B+ market growing 8-16% annually, with a genuine 30x pricing gap below quote-only incumbents and unusually good churn mechanics for a monitoring product — but the wedge is no longer uncontested and the trust cold-start is severe. Strengths: (a) The target payer demonstrably already pays for inferior alternatives — financial-vertical SMEs pay ACA ComplianceAlpha ~$600/mo/head for largely manual tooling, RIAs pay low-thousands/yr for COMPLY/RIA-in-a-Box, and enterprise RCM (Ascent ~$50K+/yr, TRRI/CUBE ~EUR30K+/yr) is explicitly out of reach, so $199-999/mo lands in proven budget territory rather than requiring new budget creation. (b) Churn risk once 'caught up' is structurally low for this category: regulation never finishes (the 14K+ updates/yr dynamic is real and accelerating with state fragmentation), and the versioned audit log is a system-of-record artifact the buyer shows examiners — cancelling means losing your defensibility record, which is genuine lock-in rather than marketing copy. This is the single best feature of the concept versus typical 'monitoring tool' churn patterns. (c) Expansion is moderate, not strong: seat expansion is structurally capped (1-3 person teams by definition), so expansion must come from jurisdiction/domain add-ons and the multi-client Firm tier — credible but not the compounding seat dynamics of a 9-10 business. Weaknesses keeping it below 8: (1) The claim 'nothing exists in the $100-500/mo slot' is false as of mid-2026 — Changeflow sells AI-filtered government-source monitoring from $99/mo with a free trial, and RegPulse, financialregulations.eu, and Luthor are publishing alternatives/SEO content in exactly this niche; the slot is being attacked now, so speed and depth (obligation map + audit log vs. plain alerting) must carry the differentiation. (2) Accuracy liability cold-start: one missed effective date or wrong applicability call is career-level damage to the exact buyer persona, and a no-brand startup must overcome the 'can I cite this to an examiner' bar that Thomson Reuters cleared by reputation. (3) The '44% track manually' stat could not be independently verified. Net: solid niche-to-midsize opportunity with believable $6-12K blended ACVs, where the Firm/consultancy tier is the real revenue engine and the audit-log moat is the real retention story.

## Recommended Revenue Model

Recommended structure: keep the three-tier shape but price the expansion axis explicitly per-jurisdiction/per-domain, since seats won't expand. (1) Core $249/mo (one domain, 3 jurisdictions, weekly digest, change cards) — priced above Changeflow's $99 entry deliberately, because the differentiator is the citation-pinned obligation map and immutable audit log, not alerting; do not race to $99. (2) Multi-state $599/mo (15 jurisdictions, Slack alerts, examiner-facing PDF/CSV audit export) with +$25/mo per additional jurisdiction beyond plan — this anchors cleanly against ACA's ~$600/mo/head while delivering automation ACA lacks. (3) Firm $1,250-1,500/mo for fractional-compliance consultancies and accounting firms (5 client profiles included, +$150/profile, white-label digests) — this is the highest-leverage tier because one consultancy aggregates 10-30 SMEs and converts your product into their billable deliverable; expect it to drive the majority of NRR. Gate the audit-trail export and version history to paid tiers — it is the retention asset. Land motion: free 30-day 'regulatory exposure scan' that generates the company's obligation map from its profile, demonstrating coverage breadth before asking for payment. Push annual prepay (2 months free) since the audit log only has value as an unbroken record. With 90%+ gross margins on government-source COGS, unit economics work even at small scale: 300 customers at a $550/mo blend = ~$2M ARR, plausibly reachable in this niche; 1,000+ customers requires winning the consultancy channel. Net revenue retention target 110-120% via jurisdiction creep (state fragmentation does the upsell work for you) and second-domain attach (e.g., lending + privacy).

## Adversarial Review — strongest case AGAINST (verdict: weakened)

The pitch's own diligence is stale, and that staleness is the tell. It lists Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence as the enterprise incumbent, but CUBE completed acquiring that business on Dec 31, 2024 (plus Oden and 4CRisk), creating a consolidated ~1,000-customer AI-investing incumbent with every incentive to ship a self-serve down-market tier. And 'zero cheap challengers found' is false-adjacent: Hadrius (YC, AI-native SEC/FINRA compliance), Bretton AI (ex-Greenlite, $75M Series B in Feb 2026), Changeflow, RegPass, and a continuous YC compliance pipeline are already attacking the SME slot from adjacent wedges with existing distribution. The window the pitch claims is empty will be crowded within 12 months regardless of what frontier labs do. Second, the margin story contradicts the trust story. The $5-15/mo COGS and 90%+ gross margin assume zero humans in the loop, but the product's entire reason to exist is that a compliance officer stakes their license on it. Applicability classification ('does this BNPL rule reach my CA lending product?') is legal judgment — scope definitions, exemptions, preemption, license-type carve-outs — and cheap-model triage WILL misclassify the long tail. The error asymmetry is brutal: false negatives (missed applicable change) are invisible until an examiner finds them, at which point the account is dead and word spreads through a small, tight community; false positives create noise fatigue until the digest goes unread and the product becomes another newsletter. The entailment check only guards against hallucinated summaries — it cannot detect omissions, which are the fatal error class. Tolerable recall on material changes is ~99%+, and the immutable audit log cuts both ways: it is discoverable evidence that the product told the customer the wrong deadline. Getting there requires expert editorial QA ($100-300/customer/mo), which is negative-margin at $199 and halves margin at $499. Third, data access degrades exactly where the pitch claims the value is. Federal sources (Federal Register API, regulations.gov, eCFR) are genuinely free and structured, but state-level fragmentation — the stated 'why now' — runs through state administrative codes that are frequently published officially via LexisNexis/Westlaw-hosted portals with restrictive terms, breaking the promised 'redline against prior rule text' for many states; 50-state legislature tracking via LegiScan is a paid annual per-state subscription above a 30K-query free tier. The '50 states x multiple agencies' adapter moat is real grinding work — which also means a 2-3 person team cannot deliver the promised 15-jurisdiction, multi-domain coverage at launch quality; shipping shallow coverage in a product whose value proposition is 'we never miss anything' is self-refuting. The honest version of this product is one regulatory domain (e.g., consumer lending), ~10 jurisdictions, human-QA'd, at $499-999/mo with 60-75% gross margins — a decent niche business, not the pitched 90%-margin land-grab. Churn is the one front that survives attack: regulation never lets you feel caught up, and the accumulated audit trail is a genuine switching cost — though 10-20%/yr base decay from SME fintech/crypto customer mortality is unavoidable.

## Recommended Tech Stack & Unit Economics

Ingestion: Federal Register API + regulations.gov API + eCFR XML (all free) for federal; agency RSS/HTML fetchers; LegiScan API paid tier (~$5-15K/yr for broad state coverage) plus Open States for legislatures; per-state adapter scrapers (Zyte/Firecrawl for hostile sites) for regulator bulletins; manual/licensed workaround needed where admin codes are Lexis/Westlaw-hosted. Pipeline: scheduled crawl (plain cron/queue or Temporal — this is a deterministic pipeline, not open-ended agency), normalized text snapshots in Postgres+S3, content-hash plus DOM-aware structural diffing. Triage: Haiku 4.5 or Gemini Flash classifying diffs against company-profile embeddings (products x jurisdictions x license types). Synthesis: Sonnet 4.6-class generating change cards with offset-anchored quote pins into stored source snapshots; separate NLI/entailment verifier pass plus deterministic validators (dates parse, citations resolve, effective dates match source text). Storage: event-sourced obligation graph in Postgres for immutable version history; PDF/CSV export service. Delivery: web app + Slack/email. Human layer (non-negotiable in year 1): editorial review queue gating high-materiality cards. Unit economics at ~100 customers: shared crawl infra $3-5/customer/mo, LegiScan amortized $4-15, cheap-model triage $3-10, frontier synthesis + verification $5-15 (50-200 deltas/customer/mo at $0.10-0.50 each) = $15-45/mo pure machine COGS — close to the pitch's claim. But expert QA at even 1-2 hrs/customer/mo adds $100-300, making realistic blended COGS $120-350/customer/mo initially, declining as the applicability classifier earns trust. Realistic gross margin: 60-75% at the $499 tier, negative at $199 with QA. Viable shape: drop the $199 tier, launch single-vertical (consumer lending or money transmission), ~10 jurisdictions, $499/$999 pricing.
